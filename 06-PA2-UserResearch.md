## 1. Research Overview

### 1.1 Research Context

In PA1, our group analyzed the Freestyle Chess website and identified several mobile usability issues, including difficult one-handed navigation, long information lists, limited search/filter support, unclear onboarding for first-time users, and unexpected video playback behavior.

In PA2, our group continues with the same product scope and conducts user research to understand how real users interact with the Freestyle Chess mobile website in realistic mobile contexts.

### 1.2 Research Goals

The user research aims to answer the following questions:

1. What information do users expect to find first when visiting a chess event website on mobile?
2. How easily can users understand the purpose of the Freestyle Chess website from the homepage?
3. What difficulties do users encounter when navigating the website on a smartphone?
4. How do users find player ratings, event news, schedules, and video/livestream content?
5. What mobile-specific problems appear when users operate the website with one hand or in short browsing sessions?
6. Which user pain points should be prioritized in later design proposals?

### 1.3 Research Questions

| Research Question | Related Website Area | Expected Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Do users understand what Freestyle Chess is about? | Home page | Survey ratings, interview comments |
| Can users navigate between major sections easily on mobile? | Navigation menu | Survey ratings, interview observation |
| Can users quickly find a player or rating information? | Rating page | Survey ratings, task observation |
| Can users find event news with enough context? | News page | Survey answers, user preference patterns |
| Does the video/livestream section match user expectations? | Videos & Streams page | Survey ratings, interview quotes |

## 2. Research Methods

Our group used two user research methods:

1. **Online survey:** Participants opened the Freestyle Chess website on their phone, explored the website for a few minutes, and answered a Google Form based on their experience.
2. **Direct interview:** A smaller number of participants were interviewed directly. During the session, they were asked to perform several tasks on the mobile website while the research team observed their behavior and asked follow-up questions.

This combination helps the group collect both broad and deep data. The online survey provides responses from more users, while direct interviews provide richer qualitative insights, user quotes, and behavioral evidence.

### 2.3 Why These Methods Were Chosen

The online survey was chosen because it allows the group to gather feedback from many users in a short amount of time. It is useful for identifying common patterns, such as how many users find the navigation difficult or how many users expect search/filter support.

The direct interview was chosen because some usability problems cannot be fully understood through survey answers alone. By watching users interact with the website, the group can observe hesitation, repeated scrolling, accidental taps, grip changes, and confusion that users may not describe clearly in a form response.

## 4. Research Procedure

### 4.1 Online Survey Procedure

Participants were asked to:

1. Open the Freestyle Chess website on a smartphone browser.
2. Explore the website for about 3-5 minutes.
3. Check important sections such as Home, News, Schedule, Rating, and Videos & Streams.
4. Return to the Google Form and answer questions based on their actual experience.

The survey included:

- Screening questions about chess interest and device usage.
- Questions about user expectations when using chess event websites.
- Rating-scale questions about homepage clarity, navigation, information finding, and one-handed use.
- Open-ended questions about frustrations, confusing areas, and suggested improvements.

### 4.2 Direct Interview Procedure

Each interview participant was asked to perform a small set of tasks on the Freestyle Chess mobile website. The research team did not explain the interface before the task, so that the participant's first reaction could be observed.

| Task | Prompt | Observation Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Task 1 | Open the website and explain what you think it is for. | Homepage clarity, first impression, onboarding |
| Task 2 | Find the section where you can view player ratings. | Menu discoverability, reachability, label clarity |
| Task 3 | Find information about a chess player or browse the rating list. | Search behavior, scrolling effort, list length |
| Task 4 | Find news related to a Freestyle Chess event. | Filter usage, event grouping, content context |
| Task 5 | Choose a video or livestream to watch. | Clickability, video behavior, user expectation |
| Task 6 | Use one hand to open the menu, go to News, then return Home. | Thumb reach, grip change, accidental taps |

During each interview, the research team recorded:

- Actions performed by the participant.
- Places where the participant paused or looked confused.
- Comments made by the participant.
- Tasks that took longer than expected.
- Any physical discomfort or grip changes during one-handed use.

The online survey collected 72 responses. The findings below summarize the main quantitative results and selected patterns from short-text responses.

### 5.1 User Expectations

| Expected Information | Number of Users | Percentage | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Latest news | 38 | 52.8% | Users expect the website to highlight fresh updates |
| Schedule | 22 | 30.6% | Schedule is useful but not the top priority |
| Match results | 46 | 63.9% | Most selected information type |
| Player information | 31 | 43.1% | Users care about player context |
| Ratings | 33 | 45.8% | Rating information is an important reference need |
| Videos or livestreams | 22 | 30.6% | Media content matters to a smaller but relevant group |
| Rules or tournament format | 16 | 22.2% | Newer users may still need explanation |

**Insight:** Users primarily want fast access to match results, latest news, ratings, and player information. In short 2-3 minute mobile sessions, many short-text answers also mentioned "kết quả", "tóm tắt", "thời gian", and "diễn biến", suggesting that users prefer concise, scannable event updates.

### 5.2 Homepage Clarity

**Insight:** The homepage is not a complete failure, but it does not fully support first-time users. This matters because 38 out of 72 respondents (52.8%) had never heard of Freestyle Chess before the research.

### 5.3 Navigation and One-Handed Use

**Insight:** The menu is visible enough, but physical reachability remains a meaningful mobile issue. Since 32 users usually hold the phone with one hand and 30 users switch depending on context, the interface should support comfortable one-handed operation.

## 7. Key User Insights

Based on the current online survey results, our group identified the following key insights. These insights will be refined after the direct interview sessions are completed.

### Insight 1: Users Prefer Fast Access to Results and Key Event Updates

**Finding:** Users usually visit a chess event website to quickly check match results, latest news, ratings, and player information.  
**Evidence:** 46 users (63.9%) selected match results, 38 users (52.8%) selected latest news, 33 users (45.8%) selected ratings, and 31 users (43.1%) selected player information. Many short-text answers for 2-3 minute browsing sessions also mentioned results, summaries, time, and important developments.  
**Meaning:** The mobile homepage should prioritize quick, scannable information rather than forcing users to browse deeply before finding useful updates.

### Insight 2: First-Time Users Need Better Homepage Explanation

**Finding:** The homepage is generally understandable, but many users still need clearer guidance about what Freestyle Chess is and what the website helps them do.  
**Evidence:** The homepage clarity score was 3.68/5. Only 26 users (36.1%) said the homepage guidance was clear, while 24 users (33.3%) said it was only partly clear and 13 users (18.1%) said it was difficult for new users. In addition, 38 users (52.8%) had never heard of Freestyle Chess before this research.  
**Meaning:** A stronger first-screen explanation or onboarding cue would reduce confusion for users who arrive without prior knowledge.

### Insight 3: Mobile Navigation Is Discoverable but Not Fully Comfortable

**Finding:** Users can generally find and understand the mobile menu, but one-handed reachability still creates effort.  
**Evidence:** The mobile menu received 3.94/5 for being easy to find and tap, while one-handed reachability received a lower score of 3.50/5. 31 users (43.1%) reported having to stretch, change grip, or use the other hand.  
**Meaning:** Navigation improvements should focus not only on visibility but also on thumb reach, physical comfort, and reducing grip changes.

### Insight 4: Users Need Search and Filtering Support for Long or Uncertain Information Tasks

**Finding:** Users often need help when browsing long lists or when they do not remember exact event/player names.  
**Evidence:** 44 users (61.1%) reported difficulty when they could not remember exact names or information. For long lists, 31 users selected filters, 28 selected pagination, 28 selected sticky search, and 21 selected search suggestions. Only 13 users selected scrolling as enough.  
**Meaning:** The website should support flexible search, event/date/player filters, and list organization to reduce memory load and scrolling effort.

### Insight 5: Video Interaction Should Follow Familiar Single-Player Behavior

**Finding:** Users expect video selection to behave like common media platforms, where selecting a new video stops or replaces the old one.  
**Evidence:** 38 users (52.8%) expected the old video to stop and the new video to play. Open-ended responses about multiple videos playing at once included negative reactions such as "khó chịu", "không tập trung", and "không tốt".  
**Meaning:** The video/livestream section should clearly indicate the active video and prevent multiple videos from competing for the user's attention.

## 11. Summary of Findings

Based on the online survey, users generally need the Freestyle Chess mobile website to support short, goal-driven browsing sessions. The most important information includes match results, latest news, player ratings, player information, schedules, and videos/livestreams.

The research suggests that the main mobile UX issues are:

1. Users need faster access to match results and important event updates.
2. First-time users need clearer explanation of what Freestyle Chess is and what the website provides.
3. The navigation menu is discoverable, but one-handed reachability still creates physical effort.
4. Long lists and uncertain search tasks require filters, sticky search, pagination, and search suggestions.
5. Video/livestream interactions should follow familiar single-player media behavior.

These findings will be updated after direct interviews are completed. They will then be used in Requirement 2 to brainstorm user problems, organize them into an affinity diagram, and generate potential design ideas.

---

## 12. Research Limitations

This research has the following limitations:

1. The online survey depends on participants' self-reported answers, so it may not capture every real interaction difficulty.
2. The direct interview sample size is limited because interviews are still in progress.
3. Some participants may not be familiar with chess websites, which can affect how they interpret the website content.
4. Participants used different phone models and screen sizes, which may affect navigation and reachability feedback.

Despite these limitations, combining survey data with direct interview observations helps the group identify common user needs and mobile usability problems with enough evidence for the next stages of PA2.
