# PA2 User Research Questionnaire - Freestyle Chess Mobile Web

**Group:** 06  
**Course:** CSC13112 - UI/UX Design  
**Product scope:** Freestyle Chess mobile website on smartphone browser  
**Research methods:** Short interview, task-based observation, and post-task reflection  

## 1. Research Goal

This questionnaire is designed to understand how users interact with the Freestyle Chess mobile website, especially when they need to find chess news, tournament information, player ratings, schedules, videos, rules, or account-related features on a smartphone.

The research focuses on mobile-specific issues identified in PA1: one-handed navigation, limited screen space, long information lists, lack of search/filter support, unclear onboarding, video playback behavior, and discoverability of interactive elements.

## 2. Participant Screening

1. How often do you follow chess news, tournaments, players, or online chess content?
   - Very often
   - Sometimes
   - Only during major events
   - Rarely or never
2. Have you ever heard of Freestyle Chess before today?
3. What device do you usually use to read sports or chess-related information?
   - Smartphone
   - Laptop/desktop
   - Tablet
   - Other
4. When using a phone, do you usually hold it with one hand or two hands?
5. In what situations do you usually check news or event information on your phone?
   - At home
   - Before sleeping
   - While commuting or walking
   - During a live broadcast
   - During a short break

## 3. Warm-Up Questions

1. When you visit a website about a chess event, what information do you usually look for first?
2. If you only have 2-3 minutes, what would you expect the mobile website to help you find quickly?
3. What makes a mobile news or sports website feel easy to use for you?
4. What usually makes you leave a mobile website quickly?
5. If a website is about "Freestyle Chess", what do you expect to see on the homepage?

## 4. Task-Based Observation

Ask the participant to use their own phone if possible. Do not explain the interface before the task. Observe where they tap, pause, scroll, hesitate, or ask questions.

### Task 1: Understand the Website Purpose

**Prompt:** Open the Freestyle Chess mobile website. Without browsing deeply, tell us what you think this website is for.

Observation notes:

- Can the participant understand the website purpose from the home page?
- Do they know what actions or sections are available?
- Do they mention confusion about the term "Freestyle Chess"?

Follow-up questions:

1. What made you understand, or not understand, the website's purpose?
2. What information should appear earlier on the homepage?
3. Did you feel the website gives enough guidance for first-time users?

### Task 2: Navigate to a Specific Section

**Prompt:** Please find the section where you can view player ratings.

Observation notes:

- Does the participant find the navigation menu easily?
- Do they use one hand or switch to two hands?
- Is the top-left hamburger menu hard to reach?
- Do they understand the text-only navigation labels?

Follow-up questions:

1. Was the navigation menu easy to reach on your phone?
2. Were the section names clear enough?
3. Would icons help you recognize sections faster? Why or why not?
4. Which navigation items felt most important to you?

### Task 3: Find a Player or Rating Information

**Prompt:** Find information about a specific chess player or browse the rating list until you find someone interesting.

Observation notes:

- Does the participant use search immediately?
- Do they scroll for a long time?
- Do they lose track of their position in the list?
- Do they need to return to the top to search?

Follow-up questions:

1. Was it easy to find a player in the rating list?
2. Did the list feel too long, manageable, or unclear?
3. Would pagination, a sticky search bar, or filters make this task easier?
4. What player information do you expect to see first?

### Task 4: Find News About an Event

**Prompt:** Find news related to a specific Freestyle Chess event or tournament.

Observation notes:

- Does the participant notice event-based filters?
- Do they understand how news is grouped?
- Do they rely on scrolling or filtering?
- Do they search for date, event name, player name, or keyword?

Follow-up questions:

1. How would you prefer to search for event news: by event, date, player, or keyword?
2. Was the filter easy to notice and use?
3. Did the page show enough context for each news item?
4. If multiple events happen at the same time, how would you expect the website to separate them?

### Task 5: Watch or Select a Video

**Prompt:** Go to the video or stream section and choose a video you would like to watch.

Observation notes:

- Does the participant understand what is clickable?
- Do multiple videos play at the same time?
- Does the participant know which video is currently active?
- Do they expect a single dedicated video player?

Follow-up questions:

1. Was the video section easy to understand?
2. What did you expect to happen after selecting a new video?
3. If two videos play at once, how would that affect your experience?
4. Would a YouTube-like single video player feel more familiar?

### Task 6: Use the Website in a Mobile Context

**Prompt:** Try using the website while holding your phone with one hand. Complete one simple task: open the menu, go to News, then return to Home.

Observation notes:

- Can the participant reach the main controls comfortably?
- Do they stretch their thumb or shift grip?
- Do they accidentally tap the wrong area?
- Do they mention physical discomfort?

Follow-up questions:

1. Which controls were hardest to reach?
2. Would a bottom navigation bar, swipe menu, or floating button be easier?
3. In what real-life situation would one-handed use matter most to you?
4. Did the interface feel safe and comfortable to use while moving or lying down?

## 5. Overall Experience Questions

1. What was the easiest part of using the Freestyle Chess mobile website?
2. What was the most frustrating or confusing part?
3. Which task took longer than you expected?
4. Did you ever feel unsure whether something was clickable?
5. Did the website feel more suitable for new users, casual users, or experienced chess followers?
6. What information should be prioritized for mobile users?
7. What feature would make you more likely to return to this website?
8. If you could change one thing about the mobile website, what would it be?

## 7. Closing Questions

1. Would you use this website again to follow chess events? Why or why not?
2. What type of user do you think would benefit most from this website?
3. What type of user would struggle most with this website?
4. What should the design team improve first for the mobile version?
